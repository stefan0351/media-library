"What's this?" Janeway asked Seven as she removed the silk panties wrapped around Seven's shoulder and lifted it high in the air.

The young Borg froze, seeing the anger in the Captain's eyes. She felt trapped in the tiny room alone with the Captain. The only way out was up the ladder and onto the main floor of the Delta Flyer.

"Umm. Those appear to be a woman's undergarment," Seven responded as casually as possible.

"I know that. But whose undergarments are these?"

Seven cleared her throat, attempting to buy time. "Well, when we were down on the planet, we did not have sufficient medical supplies, so it was suggested that I use my underwear to wrap my wound."

"Yes," Seven lied.

"I didn't know you wore underwear."

"Neither did I," Seven mumbled to herself.

"What was that?"

"Nothing. I was just clearing my throat."

Janeway eyed Seven from the corner of her eye while going to get the dermal regenerator. Seven shifted around on the medical table, uncomfortable of the Captain's gaze.

For some reason Janeway felt Seven was being dishonest, but the Borg had not lied to her since her very first days on the ship. So there was no reason to doubt her honesty now. But then again, jealously had a way of making people paranoid.

"Does that hurt?" Janeway asked Seven, while applying the dermal regenerator to the Borg's left shoulder.

"Consider it broken."

Janeway smiled at the response and pulled the dermal regenerator away.

"There, should be as good as new," Janeway said, smoothly running her hand along the newly regenerated skin.

Seven held her breath at the contact and almost let her right hand slip from covering her chest.

"Thank you," Seven replied, turning around in her seat and quickly pulling up her bio-suit.

"Here, let me help you with that," Janeway said, placing both her hands on Seven's bare shoulders before the Borg could finish covering them.

"Very well," Seven complied hesitantly.

Janeway softly let her hands drift from the top of Seven's shoulders, down along Seven's back to where the zipper was. Ever so slowly, Janeway pulled the zipper up, covering Seven's skin. At the base of Seven's neck, the Captain softly brushed away Seven's hair and finished zipping her up.

"Thank you Captain."

Seven stood up, turned around to face Janeway, and took a step back.

"I believe we should head up and assist B'Ella…Lt. Torres and Ensign Parker in flying the Delta Flyer."

Seven swallowed.

Janeway furrowed her brow, looked at Seven and finally responded, "Very well."

Seven immediately turned and headed for the ladder.

"So the ship went from burning to brand new in a matter of hours?" B'Elanna asked unbelievingly.

"Yup," Ensign Parker answered.

"Wow. So I guess Seven and I weren't hallucinating then?"

"Oh," B'Elanna blushed, thinking about the passionate lovemaking, the waterfall, and her declaration of love, "Nothing much."

"But you just said you thought you were hallucinating."

"Yeah. I did, didn't I? Well, a basket did appear out of thin air."

"Really?"

"Yup. And I guess that about…" B'Elanna stopped as she saw Seven climbing up the ladder and onto the main floor.

"About what?" Seven asked, wondering why B'Elanna was staring at her strangely.

Parker looked back and forth between Lt. Torres and Seven of Nine. Torres flinched and looked down to ground. In all her years aboard Voyager, Parker had never ever seen Lt. Torres look down when talking to anyone.

"I… Huh?" B'Elanna asked, forgetting the original question as she looked up and fell into Seven's deep blue eyes.

"You were saying 'I guess that about,' and then you stopped," Seven replied.

"Communications are still down Captain," Seven stated from the secondary console.

"Don't worry about it, we'll be back on Voyager soon enough," Janeway answered.

B'Elanna remained unusually quiet as she piloted the ship back to Voyager. Although quiet, she remained focused on every word that passed between the Captain and Seven.

"So," Janeway leaned back in her chair, "I was thinking you could join me on the holo-deck and relax after you've regenerated?"

B'Elanna clenched her left hand under the console. Seven, surprised at the offer, hesitated a moment when she noticed B'Elanna stiffen.

"I believe that would be unwise Captain," Seven answered smoothly.

"I believe that I should re-assimilate myself to my regular work schedule. After being gone so long I think routine should be my priority."

B'Elanna smirked as Seven brushed Janeway off, but was unsure as to whether Seven was serious to going back to her routine on Voyager. Would she forget everything that happened on the planet? Would she just brush that off as a momentary lapse in judgement because of the extenuating circumstances? B'Elanna wondered painfully throughout the rest of the flight.

"Very well," Janeway replied, extremely embarrassed at being rejected and even more irritated at B'Elanna's smirk.

The docking bay doors opened and the entire senior staff greeted them, but the Doctor and Tom were front and centre, fighting for position.

After seeing that no one was seriously injured, Tom playfully nudged the Doctor aside, "Looks like you won't be needed here Doc," Tom said as he held his right hand behind his back.

"Tom," B'Elanna said, noticing his strange behaviour.

"Well," Tom smiled and spread his left arm wide to his side, "Aren'tcha gonna give me a hug?"

B'Elanna eyed him suspiciously, "What's your right arm doing behind your back?"

Seven stared at Tom as well, "Yes Lt. Paris, what are you hiding?" Seven questioned from behind B'Elanna.

"Nice to see you again too Seven."

The Captain's faced glowed with excitement as if a cloud had just lifted. Down on the planet she had completely forgotten about what Tom told her before her leaving. Now it had all returned and she couldn't be happier.

Janeway looked directly into B'Elanna's eyes and grinned.

Looking at Tom's face, looking at the faces of Harry, the Doc, Tuvok, Chakotay, and Neelix, B'Elanna felt more pressure than she'd ever felt in her entire life.

B'Elanna felt all her feelings for Tom re-surface, all the times they'd spent on the holo-deck, all the times they'd eaten dinner, and all the times they'd spent breaking Starfleet regulations, "Sure," B'Elanna finally answered, a wave of guilt crashing down on her.
