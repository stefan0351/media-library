She had changed into some gray Calvin Klein boxers and a white tank top, and she looked at me funny as she made her way into the kitchen, probably wondering why I stood so awkwardly rooted in the spot that I was in.

"Janna, do you want some coffee?" she asked.

"No thanks Aaryn, I really should be going."

"Tea, maybe? How about some hot chocolate?" she continued.

What the hell, I thought. It beat going home and watching re runs of old Spanish soap operas that I could not understand anyways.

"Hot chocolate would be great!" I yelled into the kitchen.

"Make yourself at home," she yelled back. I heard the banging of cabinets and mugs as she set about her task.

 

So I sat on her soft black leather couch, sinking into it quite comfortably, as I took in the rest of Aaryn's living room. Tattoos, huh? I wondered, thinking of the many spots I would like to discover one on her.

I can be nosy when I want to be, and I definitely felt like being nosy.

She clicked hers against mine, saying, "To a better social life."

And then when I told them I was gay, they really didn't want a whole lot to do with me. I look at their money as quiet money, it is for me to live my life and do what I want, so long as I don't bring any girlfriends home, or let any of their friends know about me. Though this past Christmas Eve a few friends and I went to give out presents to sick kids at a few hospitals in the area."

"It really was Janna. It made me feel really good inside, being able to make a kid smile. I never had any kind of tradition with my own family, my parents would take off together and leave me with the nanny over the holidays, but I think that from now on I am going to make it a tradition to do that for the kids."

"Thanks," she said, grabbing my hand into hers. "But it doesn't make me feel as shitty as it used to."

"I am definitely glad to hear that," I grinned.

 

We sat there comfortably for awhile, until we finished our chocolate and I stood to leave.

"I really should go now," I said. "I'm sure you have to be up as early as I do."

"Yeah, I guess you should. So, what are you up to this weekend?" she asked as she walked me to the door.

It was then that I noticed a rectangular, dark blue box behind the couch.

"Aaryn! You have a Playstation2 and you didn't say anything?" I asked incredulously.

"Uhm, I didn't know if you were into that kind of stuff."

"A friend of mine works at a toy store, and she let all of us know when they got the shipments in. I bought it thinking that it would keep me from being bored once school was out. I like to keep myself occupied," she said.

I stood there in awe, of the blue box that is. It made me forget that she was even still in the room. Figures, I am finally in the company of a beautiful woman and all I can think about is getting my hands on her game system. No wonder I have been single for so long.
