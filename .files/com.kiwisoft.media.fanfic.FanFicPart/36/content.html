Big city. Yeah, it was big. It was big and it was far away from Sunnydale. Far away from her past...

Far away from Buffy.

She had to get away from all of that. Away from the slaying, away from the death, away from herself. She had to do that to become a new person. To become something different than what she was.

The darkness had almost taken her but she fought it and won. She fought it for Buffy and that was why she won. Faith had helped to prevent the Ascension.

In that moment in the dreamscape, her soul was redeemed as Buffy forgave her. But that didn't really matter. Faith couldn't face Buffy... her guilt wouldn't allow it.

That was over two weeks ago.

And now, like before, she had nothing. No friends, no family, and no Buffy. All she had were the stolen clothes on her back and a knife that she'd taken off some lame ass who'd made the mistake of thinking she was a helpless girl.

Studying Faith closely, Prue leaned against the wall and said calmly, "Because I think that Phoebe's partially right. You're not an innocent but you do need help. Besides," Prue paused to smile. "We could use the rent money."

"Yeah, I can get that," said Faith with a chuckle. She shoved one of her two pairs of pants into the backpack then wandered into the bathroom. "You're straight up honest," her voice floated out. "I dig that. I dig that a lot."

"Good," said Prue who had her arms folded across her chest. "Because I have a question for you."

"Shoot witchy witch," Faith said. She wandered back into the room with a handful of bathroom supplies.

"What are you running from?" asked Prue quietly. "Are you in trouble with the law? Because if you are, we have enough problems with the police thanks to our powers--"

Faith frowned at this and Prue paused on seeing it. She turned to stuff her bathroom supplies into the backpack and said lowly, "I don't have cops after me. Yeah, I messed up but it's nothing that I'll have police after me for. There are others, but I doubt that they care enough to do a chase."

"What did you do?" Prue found herself the question almost against her will. There was something about Faith that intrigued her. A sadness that couldn't be explained.

"Another story, another time," Faith murmured as she slung her backpack over her shoulder. "If you don't want me living with you now, that's totally cool. But you gotta be the one who ends up explaining it to your sister. Because I dunno if I'd survive that experience."

Laughing at this, Prue said wryly, "Forget it. There's no way I'm facing Phoebe. You're definitely moving in."

"Thanks," said Faith softly after a moment of pause.

"What are you thanking her for?"

The two turned around to see Phoebe draped in the doorway to the hotel room and seeing her, Prue quirked an eyebrow then turned back to Faith. Giving her another smile she said, "Good luck. I'll see you in the car."

"Thanks, I'm gonna need it," Faith returned the smile.

Prue chuckled quietly before she exited the room past a confused Phoebe who narrowed her gaze as she fixed it on Faith who was nearing her.

"What was that about?" asked Phoebe suspiciously.

"Nothing, sweets," drawled Faith. Taking soft hold of Phoebe's arm she closed the door to the room and said, "You wanna come with me to turn in my keys?"

"Sure," said Phoebe who was in mystification about whatever had gone on between her sister and Faith.

"And this is your room," said Piper with a smile. She turned on the light and Faith looked in a somewhat musty room that was filled with several boxes. "Sorry," Piper gave a sheepish smile. "We've been using this room as our storage space. I guess that we'll just be moving all of this stuff into the attic tomorrow."

"Cool," Faith wore a small smile as she set down her backpack and looked at Piper. "Whatever you want, I'll do." She was quiet for a moment before she shifted from foot to foot and looked at Piper with lidded eyes. "Uhm," she began hesitantly. "I just wanna say thanks. I know that you probably got something to do with this. You and Phoebe, I mean."

"It was mostly Phoebe," replied Piper while she returned the smile. Leaning on the door frame she folded her arms across her chest and said, "But I have to admit that I do think that you just might need our help. And maybe," Piper then formed a playful expression. "We just might need yours."

"Yeah," said Faith quietly. "Maybe."

Pushing herself off the door frame, Piper wore a gentle expression as she said, "You'll be safe here, Faith. With all the things we've seen and what you've probably seen as a vampire slayer, there's no reason to hide what we really are."

"Makes sense," admitted Faith who dragged her gaze away from Piper's as she walked towards the bed. "But it just isn't all that simple, is it?"

"No," Piper agreed softly. "Life never is." She studied Faith for another moment before she gave another smile and said, "Well! I should let you get to bed. It's almost two a.m."

Piper was almost out the door when Faith said, "Tell Phoebe I said thanks and goodnight, huh?"

"I will," murmured Piper before she quietly shut the door to Faith's room. She was leaning against the closed door in thought when suddenly Phoebe appeared out of nowhere to stand in front of her. Jumping slightly, Piper held her hand over her heart and exclaimed, "Phoebe! Are you trying to give me a heart attack?"

"How is she doing?" asked Phoebe, ignoring Piper's questions as she moved closer. "She's doing okay, right?"

"Faith's fine," assured Piper with a small smile. She touched her sister's cheek forehead lightly and said, "But how about you? Are you sick? You're acting strange."

"I'm not acting strange!" Phoebe said huffily as she pushed Piper's hand off her forehead. "I'm fine! Fine, I tell you. Just fine!"

Watching Phoebe walk away as she continued her claims of being fine, Piper looked to Prue who stood at the end of the hallway wearing a droll expression.

"Methinks little sister has a crush," said Prue while moved forward to stand next to Piper.

"Methinks you're right," said Piper with a smile. "And me also thinks that crush just might be returned."

"Oh?" Prue arched an eyebrow. She looked at Phoebe who was still busy ranting to herself and formed a slow smile. "This," drawled Prue humorously. "Should be fun to watch."

"Oh yeah," Piper said with a nod. "Lots of fun."
