There was silence . but this time it didn't feel so awkward. After Sam's last remark, Brooke decided that she should quit this tease and try to continue the decent conversation they were having. "Hey, you must think being a cheerleader equates to having a minuscule amount of grey matter but I'd like to prove otherwise."

Sam just opened her mouth but couldn't quite figure out what to say.

"You know one of your turn-ons . buildings." Brooke offered, smiling gently.

"Oh. Well I just like to look at buildings. Especially skyscrapers . "she started slowly. "I don't understand why either . I just find them fascinating. I would love to pick up photography . as in serious photography . when I can afford a real camera." She smiled shyly.

They stayed in silence as the jukebox played Jewel singing, " . There is this hunger. This restlessness inside of me. And it knows you're no stranger. You're my gravity. Sam froze for a moment. "But don't you have a meeting to go to?"

Brooke kept silent for a while before she broke into a suggestive smile, "If you can accidentally forget about your source, I think I can manage to accidentally forget about the meeting."

"Brooke ." Sam slowly said and Brooke turned to face her, "Shut up."

Brooke couldn't believe what she just heard and brought the car to a screeching halt. She was about to burst into a bout of expletives when Sam merely lifted her left hand indicating Brooke to wait before she reacted.

Then she pointed to her ear asking Brooke to listen. Brooke heavily leant back into her seat with lips pouted to show her disapproval . but she listened anyway.

When the song ended, Sam opened her eyes and faced Brooke who turned mimicked her actions. She raised her eyebrows as if asking, "What do you think?"

"See anyone who listens to it without much thought will think it's a happy song right? I mean with the upbeat tune, title and all ." Brooke only nodded in response.

"That's like you. You know a patchwork of paradoxes. Like this song, you're perky and bubbly and Miss Have-It-All but deep down . there's a lot of sadness, so many things that you wish you can change but . you really are just like . me."
