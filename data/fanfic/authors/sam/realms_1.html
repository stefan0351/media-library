"I'm really not sure. It came this afternoon in the mail with no return address and appears to be broken into several sections each one of them in a different language. I just now found a section that is in Babylonian that I can translate."

Joyce looked at him and smiled, she recognized the look on his face and new that he wouldn't be able to enjoy himself until he figured out what was in the book. "How about I throw something on to eat while you figure what's in that book," She gave him another quick kiss on the cheek before heading towards the kitchen.

Joyce had wanted to spend a romantic evening with Giles but realized that wasn't going to be possible until he figured out what was in the book. Early off in their relationship she had learned that when it came to books, especial really old ones, written in forgotten languages he was like a little kid in a brand new toy. He wouldn't be able to concentrate on anything else until he deciphered what he was reading. At first it had bothered her but she eventually came to love him even more for it, mainly because of the countless times that particular habit of his had saved Buffy and her friends lives.

Giles sat in his recliner, his eyes glued to the book that he held in his hands. He read the fascinating tale that lay before him.

Giles mind came to a sudden stop as he went to the next paragraph. Before him were possibly answers to age old questions that the council did not even know.

Giles laid the book down on the stand next to the recliner and headed to join Joyce. He walked slowly through his house as he made his way to the kitchen, the words of the mysterious book echoed in his mind. A thousand questions raced in his head, He now had knowledge of how Slayers, Hellsmouths, and vampires came into being but he was unsure of what to do with that knowledge. Strangely the biggest question in his mind was not about anything scribed in the book but about the signature at the end, <Who is Fey?>

Buffy sat in her and Willows dorm room alone staring at a picture of the redheaded hacker. A tear rolled down her cheek as she mentally scolded herself for not having the courage to tell Willow that she loved her. If only she would have looked her sweet redheaded friend in the eyes and said those magic words, I love you more than anything else, things might be different. But she had been afraid that Willow would not have felt the same way so she remained quite and now her secret love had found Tara and was with the blonde witch right now. Buffy felt jealously rip through the fabric of her being at the mere thought of Willow and Tara being together. What did the other girl do to deserve Willow. The slayers inner voice yelled at her mockingly, "She did what you were afraid to do and told Willow how she felt."

Another tear rolled down Buffy's cheek as she lay back in her bed mourning over the chance she had lost with Willow. She closed her eyes and began to fade into a restless sleep.

"It's not to late to tell her slayer," replied the brown haired woman that stood before Buffy.

Buffy looked at the women then at the scenery around her. She then turned back to her, "Who are you and where am I?"

"This place is not real it exist only in your dreams. As for me, I am your spirit guide but you may call me Fey."
