Monday morning came around fast. Buffy rushed around her room getting books and other essentials together.

"Mom, can you give me a ride to school?" Buffy yelled down the hallway.

"Yes, but I'm leaving right away. Get a move on." Came the reply from downstairs.

"Okay. Be right there." Buffy yelled back, checking herself in the mirror for what seemed like the hundredth time. Looking good seemed to be important today, and although she didn't want to admit it, Buffy knew why.

"Two kisses and you're turned inside out." Buffy mumbled to herself as she hurried downstairs and outside to the car.

"You seem to be in a good mood today." Joyce commented on the drive to school.

"Hm, I guess so. You know me,can't wait to learn stuff at school." Buffy said, staring out the window.

Joyce smirked affectionately at her daughter, "Uh huh, that's what I'm hoping."

As they pulled up to the school Buffy quickly said her goodbye and rushed into the building. "Two minutes to get to English. Yay mom, running that red light paid off." As she walked into the classroom, the bell rang starting another long, boring day of high school.

"You guys shouldn't be complaining about school." Willow lectured Xander, Oz, and Buffy in the cafeteria during lunch. "We only have a little while left at this place, and then we're on our own. That's scary."

Oz put a hand on Willow's arm. "Don't worry, I'll protect you."

Willow tried to glare at Oz, but it came out a smile. "That's sweet, but I'm serious. We're taking this," Willow gestured around her, "for granted! Next year we'll wish we were back here,.hanging out together."

Buffy smiled at her friend. "No worries Will, we'll still keep in touch, wherever we end up. I don't know where I'm ending up, but I'm sure I'll be able to keep up the correspondence."

Xander nodded his agreement, "Like Buff says, we'll stay in touch. You guys off to college somewhere, and me still here in good ol'Sunnydale working at some fast food place or something."

Willow punched Xander in the arm. "Stop saying that. You'll find something good. Maybe community college, or maybe your dad can get you on where he works."

Xander smirked at his best friend, and leaned back in his chair. "Nah, I think I'll stay right here. Work in the café or something. Hey, I could even be the janitor. Wouldn't Principal Snyder just love that."

"What would Principal Snyder love?" Cordelia asked as she walked up to the table. Glancing around she nodded at the group in greeting, noting the way Buffy had lit up slightly as she had walked up. She was sure her face had done the same thing.

Xander wrapped an arm around Cordelia's waist. "Hey there Cordy, I was just explaining how I may end up tormenting Snyder for the rest of his career here at Sunnydale High by working here after high school."

Cordelia wrinkled her nose up at the thought. "Aren't you going to try for something more,ambitious?"

Xander moved in closer and mock whispered in her ear, "What do you have in mind, baby?"

"Nothing like you're thinking." Cordelia replied, squirming away from him.

Changing the subject, Oz spoke up, "Are we doing anything tonight? The band is kinda practicing at the Bronze tonight, so if you guys want to come,."

"Yeah, we should all Bronze it tonight!" Willow said excitedly, "I mean if we haven't all overdosed on partying from Friday."

Buffy nodded in agreement. "I say let's Bronze it, but skip the party aspect. It's only Monday, I need to conserve my energy to make it through the rest of the week."

"I'm in." Xander said. "Cordy?"

Cordelia nodded, "Sure. Beats the hell out of homework."

That night Willow, Xander, and Cordelia sat in their usual place at the Bronze; out of the major traffic area, but still close to the stage where Oz was now performing with the band. For a Monday the place was jumping, as it seemed not every senior was partied out.

"You know, it's a little boring just sitting here not talking. Where's your girls'sense of fun?" Xander asked, bouncing in his seat a little.

Willow tore her gaze from the stage and gave Xander a frown. "You know we always wait for Buffy to finish her patrol before we all go up and dance."

"Well, you do know that it IS possible to have fun without the help of Buffy,.ouch!" Xander pulled his arm against his chest, pouting at Cordelia. "What was that for?"

Cordelia gave him a look. "You know what she meant. We wait so we can include her into the fun,whatever it is. Even I know that."

"Gee, when did you start sticking up for Buffy? Normally it would be you agreeing with me." Xander grumbled to his girlfriend.

Cordelia sighed. "Yeah well, maybe I've matured. Besides I have nothing against Buffy." That remark got stares from both Xander and Willow. "What?! I don't. Homecoming was just a small misunderstanding." Her friends continued to stare in disbelief.

"What's the looks for guys?" Buffy asked as she took a seat next to Willow.

"Hey Buffy," Willow greeted, "Nothing. Just talking."

"Yeah," Xander cut in, "Talking about how you and Cordy seem to have resolved your differences since last week."

Buffy nodded. "We have." She said simply.

"See? I told you." Cordelia said, crossing her arms.

"Well,.I guess that's good then." Xander said slowly.

"Yep." Buffy agreed, while Cordelia said nothing.

"So," Xander started, ready to change the subject, "Who's gonna get down with their bad selves on the dance floor with me?" Everyone looked at each other.

"Sure," Buffy said, "I didn't even get a workout on patrol tonight, I'm good for some calorie burning."

"Me too." Cordelia offered.

Willow was already up, and she and Xander led the way onto the crowded dance floor. Pressed together, the gang fought for a small circle of space to move. Cordelia and Xander were hip to hip, as were Buffy and Willow. As the crowd shifted when the beat became faster, so did they. Xander was working his dance moves around Willow, while Buffy found herself face to face with Cordelia in the remaining space.

Figuring Buffy didn't want her quite so close, Cordelia stepped back to give more room, but was instead pushed forward into Buffy by the couple behind her.

Grabbing Cordelia in a hug-like embrace, Buffy stepped back to prevent the two of them from falling. Cordelia slowly straightened up, losing the tight embrace in the process, but did not take her arms from around Buffy's neck.

"Thanks." She smiled, slightly embarrassed.

"No problem." Buffy said, her hands still resting on Cordy's hips.

Realizing that they weren't moving any longer, they broke contact from each other and started to dance once again, though this time neither of them bothered to keep much distance from each other.

What is the matter with me? Buffy thought. Here I am dancing with Cordelia, and wanting to get even closer than we are. If we weren't in public I'd be tempted to kiss her again. Again! What the hell brought this on, anyway?

Cordelia was having similar thoughts as they continued to move to the music. Eventually though, the crowd shifted again so that Xander and Willow were once again with them.

The group danced until the band begged off for the night, then gathered by the door to decide travel arrangements for getting home. There was a time once where they wouldn't have thought twice about walking home, but in the past year they became used to the fact that it was simply too dangerous to walk outside after dark,.even in small groups. Oz and Cordelia were the only two with vehicles, so it was only a matter of deciding who went with who. Usually Buffy avoided using Cordelia as a ride, which is why it surprised Willow and Xander, and even Cordelia when she suggested Oz and Willow take off without her.

"You'll go in Cordelia's car?" Willow asked, making sure she heard right.

Buffy shrugged, "Yeah, I realized my house is actually closer to hers than to yours. The weird things you find out sometimes,"

Willow had known this all along, but she always figured that Buffy just rather go with them then be awkward during a ride home with Cordelia.

"Alright. We'll see you guys tomorrow then." Willow gave Buffy a hug goodnight, and waved to Xander and Cordelia as she and Oz left the Bronze.

"Well ladies, shall we go?" Xander said, linking his arms with Buffy and Cordy.

"Ooh, what a gentleman." Buffy joked, as they headed outside to Cordelia's car.

"Who am I dropping off first?" Cordelia asked as she unlocked the doors. She knew Xander would be the logical choice since he lived only 5 blocks from the Bronze, but also knew he may want to be the last one dropped off in hopes of getting in a little groping action.

Buffy remained silent so Xander spoke up, "Me I guess. I'm closer. Plus it'll be faster for the two of you."

Satisfied, Cordelia nodded then started driving.

As they arrived at Xander's place, Xander leaned over and kissed Cordelia goodnight, then opened the door, pausing to wait for Buffy to make the change from the back seat to the front seat. Closing the door for her, Xander gave a cheery wave before heading up the walk to his house.

The girls sat in silence while they waited for Xander to be safely in the front door. When he was in, they continued on their way, as uneasy tension developing as neither girl could find the courage to speak their mind.

Finally, as they pulled up in front of Buffy's house, Cordelia spoke.

"I'd ask if you're mad at me, but then I don't really think you are."

Buffy turned to Cordelia in surprise, "I'm not mad at you."

Cordelia was silent for a moment. "Why did you volunteer to come with me tonight?"

Buffy looked away, "I'm sorry, did you not want me to go with you tonight? I,.I guess I could have gone with,."

"No, that's not what I mean." C ordelia interrupted. "I just meant,I don't know."

" I don't know either." Buffy said quietly. "I don't know what's going on, and I don't understand what I may be feeling."

"That's what I mean,." Cordelia said seriously. "We were just experimenting the other night,and well that other time too."

Buffy froze at Cordelia's words, the realization that her friend didn't feel the same way she did hitting her like a punch in the stomach.

"Yes, that we were." Buffy nearly whispered, as she turned to look out the side window, desperately wishing she had gone with Willow and Oz tonight.

"Well okay, then let me tell you something, even if you don't feel the same way. God, I don't even know why things get this complicated."

Buffy closed her eyes for a moment, then turned to face Cordelia again.

"I don't know what you felt when we kissed, or even what you felt tonight on the dance floor," Cordelia paused, "But I think I'm attracted to you. I don't know why I am, hell I have a boyfriend, but after we kissed,.let's just say I've been thinking about it a lot."

Buffy couldn't help the grin spreading across her face. "You don't have to worry about me not feeling the same, Cordy. Do you know how hard it was for me to let go of you at the Bronze tonight? It took one kiss and now, suddenly, I'm attracted to you in the worst way." Buffy let out a little laugh, "Who would have thought?"

Cordelia smiled, "Certainly not me. Then again I did end up with Xander as a boyfriend, so my instincts may be out of whack." She teased.

"Hey! That sounds like an insult. You know I can just," Buffy was cut off as Cordelia leaned over to kiss her, effectively shutting her up. Reaching up, Buffy slid her fingers into Cordelia's hair as Cordelia wrapped her arms around her, pulling her closer.

Several minutes later, both girls pulled back breathless. Licking her bottom lip, Cordelia grinned, "Sorry, I couldn't resist."

"No problem here." Buffy said, taking a deep breath to calm herself. "It's too bad that it's after 10 though," she said, glancing at the clock on the radio. "I have to go in before mom figures I've been killed by vamps or something."

"Yeah," Cordelia sighed, "Well I'll see you tomorrow at school then,"

Buffy nodded once. "Definitely." Leaning over, Buffy placed a quick kiss on Cordy's lips before opening the door. "Goodnight."

"Goodnight Buffy." Cordelia smiled as she watched Buffy go into her house. Driving away, Cordelia couldn't wait for the next day to begin.
