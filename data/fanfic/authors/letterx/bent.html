"I can't believe the two of them! After all we did for them, they still can't seem to get it together and just love each other." Tom complained, yet again. He so hates it when all his hard work ends up being for nothing.

"Calm down Tom. I mean all we really did was play background band musicians for Seven and Lanna. It was actually kinda fun to be up on stage like that regardless of why we ended up doing it." Harry tried to reason with his best friend. It was times like these that he wished he could have befriended Tuvok.

"But that's the thing, Harry, what we did had a purpose. Seven first came to us to ask us to be in this band so that she could finally admit how she felt about Lanna to her face. The later on, Lanna asked the same thing. Each hoped that they could then, ya know, get together. But no, Seven just had to push B'Elanna away."

"Yeah, poor Maquis. She's heartbroken. Seven never even gave her a reason why she did what she did. But why are we dwelling on this point Tom?"

"Because, it's only right that we finish our job and help them get together. Right, Chakotay?"

"Um, yeah. You're absolutely right Tom. But first, I think I need to have a little talk with Seven. Computer, end program." Tom, Harry, the Doc, and the halls of Voyager all vanished only to be replaced by the yellow line of the holodeck grid.

Chakotay stood there dumbstruck. He had set out on a personal mission several days ago to find out what had his former maquis crewmate so out of sorts. Now he knew. From the story he was able to put together, Seven had sung to a holographic B'Elanna Torres admitting her love for the woman. The 'real' B'Elanna then discovered this so she in turn sung to the 'real' Seven admitting her love for the ex-drone. All with the help of a back-up band consisting of, himself, Tom, Harry, and the Doctor. Then Seven, for some reason, pushed away from Lanna, hurting her more then anyone ever thought possible of the strong half-Klingon. So being the concerned friend that he was, Chakotay had been conducting his own little 'investigation' to try and figure out what had occured. That's when he was lead here.

"Seven has some explaining to do." 'Never mind the illegal act of creating holograms of real persons but she hurt Lanna. She had defintely better have a good explaination for all this.'

And so Chakotay stomped out of the holodeck, a man on a mission.

"Seven! We need to talk. Now!" Chakotay pushed Seven back into the turbolift she had just exited.

"Commander?" Seven questioned with her customary raised eyebrow, her voice calm not betraying the shock nor the sudden fear she felt at being manhandled by what most woud describe as a kind, gentle man.

"I know about you and Lanna. Don't even try to deny it. What I want to know now is why? Why did you break it off with her, especially without leaving her with any sort of explanation as to why?"

"It is of no concern of your's. It is personal." Seven could feel her anger suddenly boil towards the surface. How dare the 'commander' manhandle her and then demand answers of her that were truly none of his buisness.

"That's where you're wrong Seven. It is my business." Chakotay growled out through clenched teeth as he invaded Seven's personal space. "You not only broke federation law but you hurt Lanna. You hurt her so much so that I have never see her in so much pain. Not when she was in a Cardassian camp, not when she had found out her father left her, not even when she had found out that she lost almost all of her maquis friends to the Dominion."

"Perhaps the fact that Ibroke federation law is of your concern 'Commander' but do not be mistaken. My relationship, or lack thereof, with B'Elanna Torres is still none of your business." It was now Seven's turn to ground out words through clenched teeth.

"Are you so cold and callus that you're not even concerned how much you hurt B'Elanna? Perhaps everyone else was right. You're just an emotionless ex-drone that could never love anyone let alone Torres."

"Arhhhh!" Seven screamed, no longer able to hold back her emotions, those same emotions 'everyone' claims that she does not have. Rushing Chakotay, Seven slammed the big man with all of her borg might in the back wall of the turbolift. "You are wrong I do love Lanna. I do feel. I feel too much." Just as suddenly as the anger had come, the fight drained out of Seven just as quickly. Backing off of the wheezing man, Seven's back hit the wall and she slumped down to the floor. "I feel afraid." It was a whispered confession that Chakotay had just managed to catch.

"Afraid of what Seven?" The counselor's voice had calmed, no longer did he sound angry. Chakotay knew that in order for him to get through to Seven he'd have to provoke her into opening up. And now that he had accomplished that, he only wanted to help Seven overcome whatever it was that was preventing her from being with her love.

"I am afraid of many things. That I would not be adaquet for Lt. Torres, that I am still too borg in my mannerisms, that I woud repulse her with my borg implants, that I perhaps can not control my feelings for her, that I am damanged beyond repair, that I am 'destined' to be alone, that I will not know how to properly love her. I. Am. Afraid."

"But Seven, don't you realize that that is what most humans feel when entering into a new relationship with another person? Perhaps they don't have your specific fears but they still fear getting involved, opening up, and allowing yourself to become vulnerable when it comes to the one you love. You just have to be able to accept these fears and push past them. They'll never go away, so don't even ask me how to make them go away. Even when youv'e been with your partner for years, those fears are still there. It's a part of love that one must accept and move on from. That is if you truly love the person you want to be with. Cause if that's the case, I can guarantee you it'll all be worth the few negative, uncomfortble feelings. Do you love Lanna? I mean truly love her?"

"Yes, of course." Seven's head shot up as soon as she felt her hands being wrapped in a second pair of hands. She never even noticed when the Commander had moved to get close to her.

"Then, what's stopping you? You already know she loves you."

"But will she be able to accept my fears, my inadequecies? I can not be perfect for her like she is for me."

"Seven, no one's perfect, not even Lanna. Let me finish. You may think she's perfect but what about all her negative qualities? She's aggressive, hot-headed, impulsive, illogical, and the list goes on. She has imperfections just like you do but love allows us to see past those imperfections and to see the woman beneath. I'm sure she feels the same about you. Have you even discussed with Lanna about your fears?"

"No."

"I thought so. She might just suprise you and say that your fears are all unfounded, which they are."

"Really?"

"Yes, really. And I think I have the perfect way to go about testing my theory. We're going to ask Lanna exactly what she thinks of all your fears."

And so Chakotay pulled Seven to her feet, ordering the turbolift to unseal the doors, and walked out, all before Seven could possibly voice an objection.
